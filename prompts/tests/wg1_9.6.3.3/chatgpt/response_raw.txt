### Question 1 (Easy)
In the SSP1-1.9 scenario, what is the median projected global mean sea level rise by 2100?

A) 0.38 m  
B) 0.44 m  
C) 0.56 m  
D) 0.68 m  

**Correct Answer:** A) 0.38 m  
*Explanation:* The table shows that for SSP1-1.9, the total sea level rise by 2100 is 0.38 metres (median value).

### Question 2 (Medium)
What is the difference in the median contribution of thermal expansion to sea level rise between SSP1-2.6 and SSP5-8.5 scenarios by 2100?

A) 0.06 m  
B) 0.12 m  
C) 0.16 m  
D) 0.18 m  

**Correct Answer:** A) 0.06 m  
*Explanation:* For thermal expansion, the median contribution is 0.14 m for SSP1-2.6 and 0.30 m for SSP5-8.5. The difference is 0.30 - 0.14 = 0.06 m.

### Question 3 (Medium)
In 2150, under which SSP scenario is the median global sea level rise more than double the SSP1-1.9 value?

A) SSP1-2.6  
B) SSP2-4.5  
C) SSP3-7.0  
D) SSP5-8.5  

**Correct Answer:** D) SSP5-8.5  
*Explanation:* The median 2150 sea level rise for SSP1-1.9 is 0.57 m. For SSP5-8.5, it is 1.32 m, which is more than double 0.57 m. Other scenarios do not exceed this threshold.

### Question 4 (Hard)
Between 2080 and 2100, which SSP scenario shows the largest range in the rate of sea level change?

A) SSP1-2.6  
B) SSP2-4.5  
C) SSP5-8.5  
D) SSP5-8.5 (Low Conf.)  

**Correct Answer:** D) SSP5-8.5 (Low Conf.)  
*Explanation:* For 2080–2100, the range of sea level change rates is 8.6–30.1 mm yr⁻¹ for SSP5-8.5 (Low Conf.), which is the largest range among the scenarios.
