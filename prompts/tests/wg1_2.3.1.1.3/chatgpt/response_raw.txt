### Question 3 (Medium):  
Based on the HadCRUT5 data, how much larger is the observed warming in LSAT compared to SST for the period 1850–1900 to 2001–2020?  

A. 0.59°C  
B. 0.69°C  
C. 0.74°C  
D. 0.83°C  

**Correct Answer:** B. 0.69°C  
**Explanation:** From the HadCRUT5 data, LSAT warming from 1850–1900 to 2001–2020 (Col 2) is 1.44°C, while SST warming for the same period is 0.85°C. The difference is 1.44°C – 0.85°C = 0.59°C.

### Question 4 (Hard):  
The trends in the multi-dataset averages show that the rate of change in GMST decreases over shorter, more recent periods (e.g., 1880–2020 vs. 1980–2020). What does this suggest about the warming trend during earlier versus later parts of the observed period?  

A. Warming has accelerated in recent decades.  
B. Warming was faster in earlier decades than in more recent ones.  
C. Warming rates have been consistent over time.  
D. Warming trends cannot be compared across periods due to differences in observation length.  

**Correct Answer:** A. Warming has accelerated in recent decades.  
**Explanation:** The shorter, more recent periods (e.g., 1980–2020) show smaller total changes but represent faster rates of warming compared to earlier periods (e.g., 1880–2020). This indicates an acceleration of warming in recent decades.
